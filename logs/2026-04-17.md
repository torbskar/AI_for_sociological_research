## Frankfurt and Gelman additions

### Decisions

**Frankfurt paragraph added to §3.1**: New paragraph inserted after the "directional failure modes" paragraph (Cheng, Peters & Chin-Yee) and before the "None of these problems are unique to AI" generalisation. Frames the specific failure modes as instances of Frankfurt's (2005) structural truth-indifference — AI output-generating processes are closer to bullshitters than liars in Frankfurt's sense. Explicitly disclaims characterising users as bullshitters; locates the structural issue in the generator, not the researcher. References added: Frankfurt (2005) *On Bullshit*, Princeton UP; Hicks, Humphries & Slater (2024) "ChatGPT is bullshit," *Ethics and Information Technology*.

**Gelman forking paths paragraph added to §5.1**: New paragraph inserted after the Mitchell tacit-knowledge paragraph and before the query-authorship paragraph. Frames unstructured AI use as a forking-paths problem: dozens of small acceptance decisions, each locally reasonable, each unremarkable, cumulating into a paper shaped by the AI's tendencies rather than the researcher's judgment. Explicitly characterises this as "not dishonesty" but ordinary post-hoc narrativisation. Reference added: Gelman & Loken (2014), *American Scientist*.

**Two-sided diagnosis now in place**: Frankfurt (structural, AI side) and Gelman (cognitive, user side) together account for why even truth-seeking researchers using AI without explicit criteria produce epistemically unaccountable work. Structured use addresses both sides.
