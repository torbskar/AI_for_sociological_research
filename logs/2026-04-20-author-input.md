# Author input: 2026-04-20

Today's session did two kinds of work: infrastructure tidying and a substantive restructure of the outline.

The infrastructure moves — splitting the root CLAUDE.md into subfolder files, moving PIPELINE.md into literature/, keeping scripts/ at root — originated with me as concerns about context load at session start. I noticed the root CLAUDE.md had grown long enough that the agent was effectively re-reading a 200+ line instruction file every time. The split was the obvious fix, but the allocation of content to subfolders was my call: logging instructions go with logs/, draft versioning with draft/, notes workflow with notes/, and the empirical grounding checklist with literature/. I also decided against moving scripts/ — my reasoning was that scripts will include more than literature work eventually, and pre-empting that now prevents a later migration.

The version-logging script started from a bash snippet I had seen and wanted to adapt. My instruction was specific: log to file only when something changes. I did not want an entry per session. I also insisted the model-tracking step be documented in the outline and flagged as part of the final article's supplementary materials — the point is not just to run the script but to make clear to readers that this is how reproducibility is handled. When the script failed against `claude config get model` (which turned out not to work non-interactively) I directed the fix toward env-var and settings-file fallbacks rather than dropping the model field.

The substantive work was the outline restructure. This was building on four notes I had written 2026-04-19 — the spectrum argument, the pre-registration analogy limits, the self-demonstration honesty note, and the persona-prompt set — plus the synthesis of persona reviews that Claude wrote from outputs I had collected across Claude and Gemini. I asked for a suggestion before any editing, because the interaction of these four notes with the six synthesis revision actions was genuinely not obvious. When Claude proposed the restructure I gave explicit placement decisions on three points: the spectrum gets its own §3.4 rather than folding into §3.3, because the spectrum is one of the paper's more important conceptual moves and burying it in an existing subsection would hide it; the writing-as-thinking caveat goes in §4.4 because it is stage-specific to drafting and deserves to be framed as such, not as a general limitation; and the self-demonstration honesty gets split between the introduction and §5.4, because framing the whole paper reflexively from the start is more honest than saving it for the limitations section. I want readers to know up front that the paper's own writing was hermeneutic, not pre-specified — this pre-empts the philosopher-persona critique at the point where it would otherwise be most damaging, rather than reacting to it at the end.

My contribution to the §5.1 reframing was insisting that the framework be characterised honestly: it gives externalisation and auditability but it does not give the publicity and anti-manipulation guarantee of a registration-server timestamp. This is close to what the synthesis suggested but the decisive point — that auditability rather than replication is the primary epistemic claim — was important to state directly rather than leave as an implication.

For §5.4 I wanted four separate threads kept distinct: documentation-is-not-verification, verification-quality, git-and-model-version calibration, and self-demonstration. The synthesis pushed some of these as a single caveat; I wanted them pulled apart because they do different work for different kinds of sceptical reader. The competence-threshold point — that the framework is calibrated for researchers with domain expertise and is thinnest where verification risk is highest — is a scope condition I wanted explicit, not glossed. The Gelman-Loken point (forking-paths applies to the documentation process itself) is uncomfortable but the paper has to engage with it once, honestly, rather than leave it for a reviewer to raise.

The move of the four notes into `notes/used/` at the end was a direct application of the workflow I wrote into `notes/CLAUDE.md`: once a note's content has been incorporated, it is archived. I wanted this done immediately rather than left for later — the working `notes/` directory should show only live material.
