# Author contributions

The core argument — that the systematic/unsystematic distinction, not AI use versus non-use, is the epistemically relevant axis — originated with the author from prior research practice. The author contributed: conceptualization of the systematic AI authorship framework and the query authorship concept; the pre-registration analogy and the spectrum extension of it; all decisions about target journal, paper scope, and normative stance; design of the workflow, file structure, and session logging protocol; design and intellectual specification of the persona review protocol; the Frankfurt and Gelman-Loken conceptual additions; the ICMJE scope clarification and the self-demonstration reflexive framing; all structural placement decisions across draft revisions; all terminological and editorial decisions; and all redirections of AI execution toward the author's intended line of argument. AI tools assisted with literature retrieval, draft text production from the author's specifications, synthesis of review outputs, and workflow implementation.
