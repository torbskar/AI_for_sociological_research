## Conceptual development: query authorship

### Decisions

**Query authorship as intellectual authorship**: Articulated and recorded as a core conceptual contribution of the paper. The argument: in structured AI use, the primary intellectual work is often located in the query rather than the response. A well-formed query presupposes a research question, a theoretical framing, a sense of what counts as relevant, and a judgment about what is missing. This has direct implications for authorship assessment — the relevant question is not "did the AI produce this text?" but "where did the reasoning come from?" The logging system developed for this project is designed precisely to document query reasoning as intellectual contribution. Noted as likely the most novel conceptual contribution of the paper beyond the workflow itself.

**Placement**: The argument belongs in the section on structured vs. unstructured use, or the discussion of what the logging system is for. It connects to the author-input logs as a concrete instantiation: those logs exist because query authorship needs to be documented, not merely asserted.

**Policy implication**: Current AI disclosure policies treat involvement as binary (disclosed/not) without asking where human reasoning was doing the work. A documentation-based framework, as argued in this paper, makes that question answerable.

## Journal change

### Decisions

**Target journal**: Changed from Methodological Innovations (Sage) to **Sociological Science**. Reasoning: stronger journal, explicit replicability focus, no restrictive AI policy, open to methodological contributions of all types. Diamond OA — no APC.

**Framing consequence**: The paper must be framed as a methodological contribution, not a commentary. Sociological Science expects substantive scholarship; a think-piece framing would be rejected on scope grounds.

**Literature review in the paper**: Keep short and concise. The journal explicitly discourages long literature reviews. Extensive background work done here feeds the argument, not the prose.

**Replication package**: Sociological Science has mandated replication packages since April 2023 (code + data as condition of publication). The transparency artefacts produced by this project — prompt templates, skill files, R scripts, search logs — constitute the replication package. This is a strong fit and should be stated explicitly in the methods section.

**Abstract word limit**: Not specified in submission guidelines. Check Manuscript Preparation page before submission.

## Full pipeline execution — Steps 1–8

### Decisions

**Pipeline route**: OpenAlex API via `search_openalex.R` (6 query blocks × 200 records max). Replaces Scopus-based workflow from 2026-04-04 session on legal grounds.

**Abstract screening method**: Keyword/pattern matching (`screen_candidates.py`) rather than Haiku sub-agent. Sub-agents cannot execute Bash regardless of auto-accept setting; direct scripting in main session is the reliable alternative for this pipeline.

**Screening threshold**: Conservative — RETAIN when in doubt; `research_signals` override prevents exclusion of papers mentioning AI tools even if they otherwise match an exclusion pattern. Full-text screening handles precision.

**Full-text scoring**: `screen_fulltexts.py` reads first 3 + middle 2 + last 2 pages per PDF (max 9,000 chars) and scores against three weighted term dictionaries (Themes A, B, C). Composite score = sum of theme scores + 3 × number of themes hit (bonus for breadth). Outputs ranked CSVs per theme plus `fulltext_scores.csv`.

**Export quantity**: 50 papers per search-letter subfolder (A, B, C, D, E, G) = 300 PDFs total in `notebooklm_export/`. Chosen to give adequate coverage per theme; NotebookLM 50-source limit handled at upload stage by selecting per-theme top 50 from the theme CSVs rather than from the export folder.

**NotebookLM workflow**: Three separate notebooks (one per theme), each configured from `supplementary/example-notebooklm-configuration.md`. Report templates A, B, C in `literature/` provide structured query sequences with mandatory citation and source-coverage instructions.

**PIPELINE.md**: Created as the durable user guide for the pipeline. Documents all scripts, parameters, manual checkpoints, and NotebookLM steps. To be updated when scripts or steps change.

**Step 6 (PDF renaming) skipped**: Author chose to proceed directly from download to full-text screening. Rename step remains available; PDFs are identified by DOI-based names in the screened outputs.
