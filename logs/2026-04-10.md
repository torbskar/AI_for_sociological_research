**Key structural finding from Elicit**: The existing literature divides cleanly into reporting checklists (post-hoc disclosure) and practical pipelines (built-in workflow structure). Checklists dominate; pipelines are rare and concentrated in clinical research and ML/NLP. Sociology has almost nothing in the pipeline category. This is the paper's gap.

**Three empirical anchors identified** for the epistemic argument:
- Zeng et al. (2025, AIRepr, EMNLP): structured prompting correlates with *better accuracy*, not just better transparency — the payoff is epistemic, not merely procedural

- Barrie et al. (political science): LLM variance "unacceptably high" even with temperature control — versioning/logging is necessary
