## Conceptual reframe and workflow updates

### Decisions

Primary term changed: "systematic AI authorship" replaces "structured AI use" as the overarching concept. "Query authorship" is retained as a sub-category or instance. Rationale: the new framing makes the authorship contribution claim explicit — systematic AI use is de facto authorship, and the execution (what the AI generates) is not the most important element.

Key distinction reframed: systematic vs. unsystematic AI authorship (not structured vs. unstructured, not use vs. non-use). Unsystematic work may be excellent but is unaccountable and unreliable. The binary permit/prohibit journal policy problem is explained by the fact that unsystematic authorship can produce bullshit at scale — reinforcing p-hacking, HARKing, and papermill dynamics that already existed.

Open science link strengthened: systematic AI authorship is an open science practice. Open science norms become more important, not less, as AI use scales.

New chapter needed: a dedicated section on pipelines and workflows — how to organise a research project systematically — is now required. This was previously folded into Section 4 but warrants more prominent treatment under the new framing.

Workflow infrastructure: global CLAUDE.md updated by author. A .gitignore added covering a git-folder, PDFs, and copyright-sensitive/large files to avoid accidental republication and reduce token and storage costs. This is itself a structural change to the workflow and should appear in the replication package documentation.

Session-start protocol added to project CLAUDE.md: at the start of each new session, read prior log and author-input, write a summary as `logs/YYYY-MM-DD-session-summary.md`, then ask whether to run `/clear`. Rationale: all needed state is on disc; regular context clearing prevents drift and reduces token costs.

File structure updated in CLAUDE.md: `logs/` listing now explicitly shows all three per-session file types (decision log, author-input, session-summary).
