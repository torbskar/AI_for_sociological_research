# Note: Distinction between working logs and final supplementary materials

## The point

The paper has developed dynamically — back and forth, iterative, with decisions revised and refined. The logs and author-input files capture this faithfully. The final supplementary materials should be tidy, reflecting the end-state: final skill configurations, final file structure, final search terms and scripts, as actually used.

These are two different things serving two different functions, and the paper should be clear about that.

## Functions

**Working logs** (daily log files + author-input files):
- Document the process as it happened — decisions made, alternatives rejected, reasoning at the time
- Capture the human intellectual contribution and the human-AI division of labour
- Honest record of iteration and development — not a cleaned-up retrospective
- Analogous to a lab notebook or research diary

**Final supplementary materials** (skill files, scripts, search strings, CLAUDE.md):
- Present the end-state artefacts in reusable, accessible form
- What another researcher would need to adopt or verify the workflow
- Tidy by design — reflect what the process converged on, not every step along the way

## The one tension to watch

The paper argues that structured use forces articulation of decisions *during* the process — analogous to pre-registration. If the supplement shows only a polished end-state, a careful reader might ask whether these configurations were genuinely in place throughout or assembled post-hoc.

The answer: the logs carry the evidentiary weight for process integrity. The supplement carries the weight for replicability. Both need to be present (or referenced) for the full claim to hold. The paper should be explicit that these serve distinct functions — the logs show *that* the process was structured; the supplement shows *what* it produced.

This mirrors how pre-registration works in practice: the registered plan (process document) and the final methods section (tidy end-state) are both legitimate and serve different purposes. Neither substitutes for the other.

## Implication for the methods section

The methods section should acknowledge the iterative nature of the workflow development — structured use does not mean predetermined or linear. The logs document this iteration transparently. The supplement presents the outcomes. Both accompany the paper.
