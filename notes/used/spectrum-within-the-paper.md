# Note: The spectrum within this paper's own workflow

**Source:** Author reflection (2026-04-19)

---

## Not all parts of this paper are equally systematic

The self-demonstration this paper offers is not of a uniform level of systematicity
throughout. Different stages of the work sit at different points on the spectrum, and
this is worth being explicit about — both for honesty and because it illustrates the
spectrum argument more concretely than a uniform claim would.

## The most systematic stage: literature search and screening

The literature search and screening is the most rigorously systematic part of the work.
Boolean search strings were documented; OpenAlex was used for cross-disciplinary coverage;
records were screened at scale (over 1,400 records); PDFs were retrieved and organised
into themed subfolders; screening decisions were logged. This is closer to the systematic
review end of the spectrum — it could straightforwardly be built out as a formal
systematic review, scoping review, or narrative review following established protocols
(PRISMA, ENTREQ, etc.).

The specific purpose here was to search across all disciplines rather than just sociology,
and to process a large enough corpus to reduce the risk of missing something important.
The systematicity was in service of coverage, not of a formal review methodology — but
the infrastructure is compatible with the latter.

Some limitations apply: the screening rubric was developed iteratively, not fully
pre-specified before the first record was seen; the corpus was not registered before
screening began. So even this most systematic stage falls short of a fully pre-registered
systematic review. But it is substantially more systematic than a narrative literature
review conducted through ad hoc searching.

## Also systematic: the review routines

The review stage — including the reviewer skill, the fresh-session design, the cross-model
persona approach developed in this session, and the synthesis protocol — is also rather
systematic. The persona prompts are documented, the synthesis protocol is explicit, and
the outputs are saved and traceable. This is a form of structured peer-review simulation
that, while not a formal review, follows documented procedures.

## Less systematic: argument development and drafting

The conceptual development of the paper's argument — the theoretical moves, the framing
decisions, the connections between ideas — was more hermeneutic. Thinking developed during
the work. Some of the most important additions (Frankfurt, Gelman, the spectrum argument,
the pre-registration reframing) were not pre-specified; they emerged from the process.
The drafting stage similarly involved iterative development rather than execution of a
fixed plan.

## What this illustrates

This internal variation is not a failure of the systematic approach — it is what the
spectrum argument predicts. A single research project can and typically will involve
stages at different points on the spectrum: highly systematic where coverage and
consistency matter most (literature search, formal screening, structured review), more
fluid where the work is genuinely exploratory or hermeneutic (theoretical development,
argument construction, drafting).

The systematic infrastructure — session logs, author-input files, reviewer skills,
project configuration — supports all stages regardless of where on the spectrum they
fall. What varies is the degree of pre-specification and the degree to which outputs
are constrained before the work begins.

## Implication for the paper's self-demonstration

The paper's self-demonstration is most credible if it is honest about this internal
variation. Claiming uniform systematicity throughout would be inaccurate and would
make the demonstration vulnerable to any reader who examines the logs. Acknowledging
that the literature search and review stages are the most systematic, while the
argument development and drafting were more fluid but still infrastructurally supported,
is both accurate and illustrative of the framework's actual value: it works across the
spectrum, not only at the pre-registered extreme.
