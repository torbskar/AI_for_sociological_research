## FLAG: Review v8 cuts against v7 before any further drafting

The cuts made to produce v8 (from ~13,600 to 9,095 words) may have been too strict in places. Certain passages in v7 or earlier versions were substantively better — not just longer — and may need to be restored or reworked rather than kept in their cut form.

**Before any new drafting session begins, this must be addressed explicitly:**

1. Do a targeted comparison of v8 against v7 (and if needed v6 or v5) to identify passages where the cut version is weaker in substance, not just shorter.
2. Decide which passages warrant restoration or reworking, and how to accommodate the word count.
3. Only proceed with drafting once this review has been done and a decision made.

**Do not treat the v8 text as the authoritative base without this check.**

This is a gate condition: no drafting before explicit consideration.
