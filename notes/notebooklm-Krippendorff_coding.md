### Krippendorff’s Location of Intellectual Contribution

Krippendorff emphasizes that for content analysis to be scientifically valid, it must be **replicable**, meaning that different people working at different times must be able to reach the same results when using the same instructions. This requirement leads to several key points in the source that support your argument:

* **Interchangeability of Coders:** The methodology aims to standardize processes so that independent observers (coders) are **interchangeable**. If the data is reliable, the specific identity of the person applying the criteria should not affect the outcome.
* **Coding Instructions as the Standard:** **Coding instructions** are the vehicle for this standardization. They are designed to "standardize processes of recording, categorizing, or valuing phenomena" so that the analyst’s knowledge is operationalized before the actual data generation begins.
* **The Analytical Construct:** The analyst’s true intellectual work is formalized in the **analytical construct**, which is a "computable model" of how the analyst believes the text relates to the research question within a specific context.

### Comparison with "Query Authorship" and AI

While the source does not mention the "Cochrane standards" (which are external to these materials and would need independent verification), your concept of **"Query authorship"** in the context of AI relates closely to several shifts Krippendorff identifies:

* **The Idea of Computation:** Krippendorff notes a "conceptual revolution" involving the **increasing replacement of communicators by algorithms**. In this new environment, messages are no longer just understood by humans but have "computable consequences".
* **Analyst as Designer, AI as Executor:** In computer-aided text analysis (CATA), the analytical task is divided: humans do what they do best (designing the logic), and computers process strings at high speeds. Your argument regarding "Query authorship" suggests that the **intellectual product is the query itself** (the instruction), while the AI serves as the new "executor" or coder.
* **The Problem of Transparency:** A challenge highlighted in the source is that how software transforms raw text into abstract representations is often **uncertain**. Krippendorff argues that for these "computer aids" to be valid, they require transparency so the analyst can trace and justify the transformations the texts undergo.
* **From Crowdcoding to Algorithms:** The source mentions **crowdcoding** (outsourcing coding to anonymous internet users) as a way to handle large volumes of data reliably. Transitioning from "crowdcoding" to AI-driven "query authorship" follows the same procedural logic: moving the labor away from the individual analyst while keeping the **authorship of the criteria** firmly in their hands.

In summary, the source supports the idea that the "intellectual product" in rigorous content analysis is the **methodological protocol** (the instructions or queries), which remains constant regardless of whether the executor is a human coder, a crowd, or an algorithm.
