# Human bias as honest qualification

## The point

Several critiques of E&W's Paper Factory apply with some force to systematic-but-human-verified AI use as well: tacit residue, systematic bias, status quo effects. The difference is not that human verification is unbiased — it is not — but that human bias differs from LLM bias in kind, and the human-in-the-loop creates intervention points where bias can be caught and corrected.

Three properties distinguish human bias from LLM bias in this context:

**Epistemic legibility.** Human bias has roughly knowable sources: disciplinary training, theoretical commitments, methodological tradition. LLM bias emerges from training corpora whose composition and weightings are not fully disclosed and whose directional effects are harder to predict or contest. Human bias is more legible and therefore more contestable.

**Correctability.** When the researcher reviews an output and overrides it, that decision can be documented, challenged by reviewers, and revised. The documentation package makes human decisions traceable. The model's internal processing at inference time is not traceable in the same way.

**Genuine triangulation.** Human bias is different from LLM bias, which means human verification provides a different epistemic signal rather than correlated confirmation. The shared-training-corpora problem undermines cross-model validation because the bias sources overlap; human verification does not have this property. A researcher bringing their own disciplinary perspective to verification is not adding noise — they are adding a different kind of signal from a different epistemic position.

## Where this belongs in the paper

"What systematic use cannot guarantee" section. The section already acknowledges that verification depends on researcher competence and is thinnest where risk is highest. One sentence should be added: that human verification introduces human bias, but that this bias is more legible, correctable, and epistemically distinct from LLM bias than the alternative. The concession is honest and actually strengthens the argument — the case for human-in-the-loop was never freedom from bias but the properties that make bias manageable.

## Relation to E&W critique

The assessment note (EandW-defence-assessment.md) concludes the paper does not need to go further in defending human-in-the-loop against E&W. This qualification is consistent with that: it is an internal honest concession, not an additional line of attack against E&W.
