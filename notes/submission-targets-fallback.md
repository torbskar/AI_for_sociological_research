# Fallback submission targets

Ordered by fit with the paper as currently written. Notes on reframing requirements are included where relevant. The default assumption is that the paper's core argument and register are preserved; what varies is emphasis and positioning.

---

## Tier 1 — limited reframing required

**Social Studies of Science (SSS)**
The strongest alternative. The tacit knowledge argument (Polanyi, Collins, Mitchell et al.), the infrastructure framing, and the epistemic authority themes are standard STS territory. Query authorship would need to be positioned as an *epistemic object* in STS terms rather than as an authorship standard, and the operational workflow detail should be reduced slightly. The policy prescription survives but should be framed as implication rather than proposal. Word limit around 8,000–10,000 words; no cutting required.

**Episteme**
A peer-reviewed philosophy journal covering social and formal epistemology. The core argument — authorship as an accountability problem, tacit knowledge externalisation, the pre-registration parallel as epistemic rather than procedural — is Episteme territory. Query authorship would need firmer positioning within epistemological debates; the policy section could be shortened. Less institutional reach than SSS but philosophically literate readership. Word limit approximately 10,000 words.

---

## Tier 2 — targeted reframing

**Science, Technology, and Human Values (STHV)**
More policy-tolerant than SSS; the normative prescription and the infrastructure argument (project folder as epistemic infrastructure, open-science compatibility) map well onto STHV concerns. STS positioning is necessary but the journal accepts direct prescription more readily than SSS. The paper's current framing requires minimal adjustment beyond the introduction. Word limit around 8,000–10,000 words.

**Sociological Methods & Research (SMR)**
Has published closely related work (Davidson and Karell 2025). The paper would need to foreground the methodological contribution — query authorship as a standard for AI-assisted social science practice — and reduce the philosophical framing. The workflow section becomes the core rather than the illustration. One risk to manage: SMR reviewers may ask for empirical demonstration of the framework's performance. The paper does not provide this and should not be rewritten to simulate it; the cover letter should position the contribution as conceptual-methodological rather than applied.

**Research Policy**
Covers science and technology governance at the intersection of economics, sociology, and management. The policy implications section maps well; the open-science infrastructure argument fits the journal's interests. Would need an introduction reframe toward science governance rather than individual authorship. Broader policy reach but lower disciplinary prestige from a sociology standpoint.

---

## Tier 3 — strategic fallback

**Accountability in Research / Research Integrity and Peer Review**
The direct audience of editors, research integrity officers, and publishers — the people who would implement the documentation package requirement. Lower prestige but potentially high practical impact. The paper would likely be read as a proposal rather than a conceptual contribution, which inverts the intended register; this is the main trade-off. Requires minimal structural reframing.

**Royal Society Open Science**
Interdisciplinary, open-access, relatively fast turnaround. The open-science infrastructure section maps well; Peters and Chin-Yee (2025) is already published there. Needs a cross-disciplinary introduction but minimal structural changes. Word limit flexible; APC applies.

---

## Standing notes

- Social Epistemology's 6,000–8,000 word limit is tight; most fallback venues allow more, removing pressure to cut if revisions are needed between submissions.
- For Tier 1: adjust cover letter and introduction framing only; core argument unchanged.
- For Tier 2: targeted reframing of introduction and conclusion; the body of the paper is stable.
- SMR is the only venue where structural changes to the paper itself would be substantial.
- SSS and STHV together form a natural fallback sequence if Social Epistemology does not work out.
