# Review Response (Prof. Martin Delacroix)

## 1. Arguments needing sharpening

### (a) Systematicity and epistemic superiority

The central claim that systematic AI use is epistemically superior is
overstated. The paper conflates auditability with epistemic quality.
Visibility does not guarantee correctness; it guarantees legibility
within a specific evaluative framework. The argument should be qualified
accordingly.

### (b) The pre-registration analogy

The analogy to pre-registration is unstable. Pre-registration is public
and binding, whereas systematic AI workflows are private and
retrospective. Logs do not function as equivalent constraints. The
analogy should be weakened or more rigorously justified.

## 2. Key caveat to add

The author should acknowledge that systematic AI authorship privileges
codifiable reasoning and may marginalize tacit, interpretive, and
abductive forms of sociological knowledge.

## 3. Most interesting argument

The concept of *query authorship* is genuinely interesting. It relocates
intellectual labour from textual production to the structuring of
outputs, opening a productive line of inquiry into infrastructural
authorship.

## 4. On scope limitation

The stated limitation is insufficient. The framework has normative
implications that extend beyond its stated domain and risks redefining
evaluative standards across sociology. This must be explicitly
acknowledged.
